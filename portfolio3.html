Number Systems
  • What are Number Systems? - Number systems are structured methods of representing numbers using specific digits or symbols. They provide a systematic way to express numbers from a defined set, ensuring each number has a unique representation that reflects its arithmetic properties. These systems also facilitate mathematical operations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Types of Number Systems

  • Binary Number System uses only two symbols '0' and '1,' to represent numbers. The name "binary" is derived from "bi," meaning two, reflecting tis reliance on these two symbols to denote various numerical values.
  • Uses/Significance
    • Enhances Computer performance and efficiency by streamlining the processing of information. It also simplifies circuit logic and serves as the basis for Boolean algebra, offering mathematical representations of logical operations.
  • Octal Number System is based on 8 and includes the digits 0 through 7. Although less commonly used today, it remains relevant in specific programming scenarios and has a close relationship with the binary system.
  • Uses/Significance
    • It is commonly used in programming as it provides a compact way to represent binary numbers, with each octal digit equating to three binary digits. This simplifies calculations and reduces the likelihood of errors.
  • Hexadecimal Number System, based on 16, is widely used in computing and digital electronics. It employs 16 symbols, consisting of the numbers 0 to 9 and the letters A to F, offering a compact way to represent binary data.
  • Uses/Significance
    • Programmers utilize hexadecimal to simplify binary representation, as each hexadecimal digit corresponds to four binary digits. While computers process data in binary, humans rely on hex for easier interpretation and management of binary numbers. Additionally, hexadecimal notation is more compact and efficient.
