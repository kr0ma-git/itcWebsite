Data & Information
  • Data consists of symbols that can be measured or described, representing specific meanings. A single piece of data, or a datum, is a representation of concepts or entities, stored in a way that can be communicated, interpreted, or processed by humans or automated systems. (Wellisch, 1996)
  • Information consists of meaningful signs that can lead to the generation of knowledge. It is a message with valuable content that provides context or input for decision-making or action. Information can come from both current communication and past data (processed or reconstructed). Ultimately, it aids in decision-making, problem-solving, and identifying opportunities.

Information Systems

  • Information Systems (IS) include various technologies like computers, software, databases, communication networks, and mobile devices. These systems are designed to carry out specifci tasks, interact with stakeholders, and deliver information in different contexts. IS consists of components for collecting, storing, processing data, and providing information, knowledge, or digital products.
  • Businesses rely on IS to manage operations, engage with customers and suppliers, and stay competitive, while also supporting supply chains and electronic markets.
  • IS Support Systems:
    • Transaction Processing System (TPS) - is essential for supporting business operations such as deposits, withdrawals, shipping, customer billing, order entry, and order placements.
    • Office Automation System (OAS) - involves computers, communication technology, and personnel to handle official tasks. It manages office transactions and supports activities at all organizational levels, including both managerial and clerical functions.
    • Knowledge Work System (KWS) - is a specialized tool that enhances knowledge creation and the application of technical skills within a business. It helps workers generate and share new insights using graphics, communication, and document management tools.
    • Management Information System (MIS) - manage daily tasks and track performance to align with organizational goals. They support middle managers and supervisors in decision-making, planning, and workflow control. MIS also collects transactional data from Transaction Processing Systems, organizes it, and presents it in reports and displays.
    • Decision Support System (DSS) - is an interactive, computer-based system that assists middle managers in decision-making by providing the necessary information for informed and effective decisions.
    • Executive Support System (ESS) - is similar to a Management Information System but tailored for executive-level decision-making. It supports decisions on company-wide issues, which require greater insight and judgment.
